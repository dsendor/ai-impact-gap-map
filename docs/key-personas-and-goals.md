# Key Personas & Goals — AI Impact Gap Map Dashboard

> Source: Notion — [Key Personas & Goals](https://www.notion.so/437de559533b420e9d5c4f543573808e)

Concise persona definitions for the AI Impact Gap Map dashboard. Each persona has a **primary question**, **what they need from the tool**, and **success = they can do X**.

---

## 🎯 1. Philanthropists & Donors
*Foundation program officers, philanthropists, EA-aligned donors, giving advisors*

**Primary question:** "Where should my next dollar go to maximize positive impact from AI?"

**Core motivation:** Pure impact maximization. Return on investment is measured in lives improved, harms prevented, or systems changed — not financial return.

**What they need:**
- Ranked opportunities by impact potential vs. current investment level
- Gap classification telling them *what kind* of philanthropic action is needed (fund research, fund deployment, fund measurement infrastructure)
- Cross-domain comparison — healthcare vs. climate vs. education in one view
- Evidence confidence so they can calibrate between "proven and ready" vs. "high-potential but speculative"

**Key criteria:** Impact scale, evidence strength, how underinvested the area is, and whether philanthropic dollars specifically (vs. commercial capital) are what's needed.

**Success:** A foundation program officer identifies that AI tutoring measurement infrastructure is critically underfunded, sees that commercial capital won't fill this gap because there's no revenue model, and directs a grant toward it.

---

## 💰 2. Impact Investors & Funders
*Impact investors, venture philanthropists, blended-finance fund managers, ESG-focused funds*

**Primary question:** "Where can I generate financial returns *and* measurable positive AI impact?"

**Core motivation:** Blended value — financial sustainability or return *alongside* impact. They need opportunities where the business model works, not just where the need is greatest.

**What they need:**
- Opportunities where strong evidence + high impact intersects with a viable revenue or exit path
- Investment Level data to spot underinvested areas where commercial capital could flow
- Gap types that signal investable moments ("Needs Capital" with strong evidence = deployment-ready; "Needs Evidence" = too early for commercial capital)
- Comparison with where commercial AI investment is *already* heavy (to avoid crowded markets)

**Key criteria:** Evidence strength, market readiness, whether the gap is addressable by commercial capital, and whether the return profile justifies the risk.

**Success:** An impact investor sees that AI-driven emissions monitoring has strong evidence and minimal commercial investment, recognizes the CBAM compliance market as a revenue model, and funds a startup in the space.

---

## 🏛️ 3. Government Officials & Policy Leaders
*Policy advisors, agency program managers, legislative staffers, regulatory body members*

**Primary question:** "Where should public investment, regulation, or incentives be directed to ensure AI creates societal good?"

**What they need:**
- Evidence of harms *and* benefits side by side — they need both to set policy
- Gap types that translate to policy levers ("Needs Evidence" → fund research grants; "Needs Capital" → create incentive programs; harm trajectories → regulate)
- Domain-level summaries they can brief upward
- Credible methodology they can cite in policy documents

**Success:** A policy advisor uses the dashboard to identify that AI in education has billions in commercial investment but almost zero public investment in outcome measurement — and builds a grant program around it.

---

## 🔬 4. Researchers & Academics
*Academic researchers, think tank analysts, evidence synthesis teams*

**Primary question:** "Where are the biggest evidence gaps I could study?"

**What they need:**
- Filtered view of "Needs Evidence" opportunities with high impact potential
- Clarity on *what kind* of evidence is missing (RCTs? longitudinal data? implementation studies?)
- Source links and existing evidence to build on
- Cross-domain view to spot shared methodological gaps

**Success:** A researcher identifies an underserved evidence gap, sees what's already been done, and designs a study proposal that directly addresses a high-impact question the dashboard surfaced.

---

## 🧭 5. AI-for-Good Newcomers & Career Changers
*Professionals exploring mission-aligned AI work, EA community members, tech workers seeking impact*

**Primary question:** "I want to help — where would my skills matter most?"

**What they need:**
- Simple, scannable overview of where AI is helping and where it's falling short
- Opportunities mapped to *types of contribution* (technical skills, research, advocacy, funding)
- Evidence that the gap is real and not already being addressed
- Inspiration without overwhelm

**Success:** A software engineer explores the dashboard, discovers that AI tutoring measurement infrastructure is critically underfunded, and either pivots toward building it or funds someone who can.

---

## 📣 6. Thought Leaders & Communicators
*Journalists, authors, conference speakers, podcast hosts, newsletter writers*

**Primary question:** "What's the real story about AI's impact — with evidence I can cite?"

**What they need:**
- Surprising, citation-worthy findings (e.g., "27 of 42 impact pathways have minimal or no investment")
- Benefits and harms assessed with equal rigor — not cheerleading or doomerism
- Specific examples that illustrate broader patterns
- Exportable data or shareable views

**Success:** A journalist writing about AI hype uses the dashboard to show that 80% of AI investment flows to areas with speculative evidence, while proven opportunities sit unfunded — and cites the gap map as the source.

## Persona Priority for Dashboard MVP

| Priority | Persona | Rationale |
|----------|---------|-----------|
| **P0** | Philanthropists & Donors | Core audience. The gap map's primary value is surfacing where philanthropic dollars are most needed. |
| **P0** | Impact Investors & Funders | Same data, different lens. Need to validate that the dashboard serves both motivations. |
| **P1** | Government & Policy Leaders | High leverage — policy decisions affect entire sectors. Similar data needs to funders. |
| **P1** | Thought Leaders | Force multiplier — they amplify the tool's reach. Need compelling visuals and shareable data. |
| **P2** | Researchers | Valuable but narrower use case. Serve well with evidence gap filters. |
| **P2** | AI-for-Good Newcomers | Important for ecosystem building but less urgent for MVP validation. |
